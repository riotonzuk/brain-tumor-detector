AI Based Brain Tumor Detection

Try Now!

Most brain tumors are not diagnosed until after symptoms appear.

TAKE A MOMENT AND THINK ABOUT THAT

A tumor can grow in the brain and go relatively unnoticed for a great period of time. When symptoms do arise, they are often very generalized and could be caused by other conditions. In fact, many different types of brain tumors exist. These tumors account for one in every 100 cancers diagnosed annually in the United States. Given the clinical significance, numerous attempts at developing computer-assisted automation tools for tumor detection have been reported, but many of the recent techniques are either manual, slow, or suffer from poor accuracy, especially for irregular tumor shapes and sizes. Currently, developing fully automated deep-learning methods with both a high level of accuracy and efficiency for improved clinical management of multiple types of tumors is holding tremendous research attention.

Here, our team aimed to develop and evaluate a fast and fully-automated deep-learning algorithm for accurate tumor segmentation and detection in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scans.

Types of Tumors

Glioma: These tumors are one of the most common brain tumors. They are often found near the supportive glial cells that surround and protect nerve cells. A glioma can affect your brain function and be life-threatening depending on its location and rate of growth.

Pituitary: Tumors that grow in the pituitary gland in the skull, below the brain and above the nasal passages. Although pituitary tumors are non-cancerous, they can have very dangerous effects if left untreated.

Meningioma: A usually noncancerous tumor that arises from the membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord. Similarly to Pituitary tumors, these tumors are usually not very dangerous by itself, however, if left untreated, can have very serious consequences.
